Street-Art Hunting

Athens boasts a number of fantastic locations for spotting huge, elaborate murals and thought-provoking pieces of artwork if you know where to look. Psirri, the university campus in Zografou, Polytechnio, and the grounds of the Fine Arts Academy are good places to start (the university grounds are advisable to visit on a Sunday when they aren’t so crowded), but there are several other spots throughout the city. Some of the best local street artists include SimpleG, Mister Achilles, and Wild Drawing, all of whom can be followed on Instagram. They won’t usually tell you the precise locations - you have to figure it out for yourself through photos and comments. And that’s the thrill of it!

Camping on the Islands

Rent a scooter or a quad bike, grab a tent, and enjoy the range of camping sites and beaches the Greek islands have to offer. While I’ve been to some of the more popular options out there, for example Rhodes, I prefer to stay away from the tourists and get close to nature. Paros and Antiparos are perfect if you love vast, open, blue seas - however, they’re quite barren and camping in the scorching heat without much shade was sometimes an issue. Overall, my favourite camping spots were in Zakynthos and Kefalonia. You can visit their beautiful attractions such as Shipwreck Beach and Melissani Cave during the day, and return to a peaceful campsite next to the sea and with great swimming pool facilities during the evening. For a quick weekend getaway close to Athens, Agistri is spectacular - especially off-season, around April or May. Visit Haunted Sites. We recommend both the caves at Pendeli and the abandoned hospital at Parnitha for a spooky twist to your trip, with both claiming many a paranormal encounter over the past decades.

Spend time in the Anarchist Neighbourhood of Athens

The central Athens neighbourhood of Exarchia has long been a central hub for anarchist activity in the area. Bustling with artists, musicians, and activists, there’s never a shortage of things to do, places to eat, and people to meet. In regards to food and drink, you can enjoy a 2 euro daiquiri (or equally cheap rakomelo, tsiporou, and other local beverages) at Dr Feelgood’s by the square, or grab a souvlaki, falafel, or delicious stuffed jacket potato nearby. As for things to do, you could participate in a self-organized workshop at Polytechneio, check out the music and events going on at the community-led center ‘Nosotros’, or visit a local squat’s open kitchen and enjoy getting to know the residents. Get Lost in Athens’ quirky, colourful, winding streets. Have a Greek coffee, enjoy some wine, grab a souvlaki wrap, and meander through Athens’ many narrow lanes. Psirri and Plaka are good places to start. Catch some stunning views. Greece has many mountains to climb and find gorgeous sights, and you don’t have to go far out of Athens to find them. Start with some smaller inner-city hills such as Strefi, Filopappou, and Lycabettus before working your way up to the more challenging Ymittos and Parnitha.
